<a href = "#chapter/book19--chapter7.txt"><span class="icon icon-forward"></span></a></p>
<div class = "bible">
<sup class="versenum">1</sup> (For the Chief Musician; on stringed instruments, set to the Sheminith. A Psalm of Dawad) O تتراجراماتون, rebuke me not in thine anger,
<br>
Neither chasten me in thy hot displeasure.
<br>
<sup class="versenum">2</sup> Have mercy upon me, O تتراجراماتون; for I am withered away:
<br>
O تتراجراماتون, heal me; for my bones are troubled.
<br>
<sup class="versenum">3</sup> My soul also is sore troubled:
<br>
And thou, O تتراجراماتون, how long?
<br>
<sup class="versenum">4</sup> Return, O تتراجراماتون, deliver my soul:
<br>
Save me for thy lovingkindness’ sake.
<br>
<sup class="versenum">5</sup> For in death there is no remembrance of thee:
<br>
In Sheol who shall give thee thanks?
<br>
<sup class="versenum">6</sup> I am weary with my groaning;
<br>
Every night make I my bed to swim;
<br>
I water my couch with my tears.
<br>
<sup class="versenum">7</sup> Mine eye wasteth away because of grief;
<br>
It waxeth old because of all mine adversaries.
<br>
<sup class="versenum">8</sup> Depart from me, all ye workers of iniquity;
<br>
For تتراجراماتون hath heard the voice of my weeping.
<br>
<sup class="versenum">9</sup> تتراجراماتون hath heard my supplication;
<br>
تتراجراماتون will receive my prayer.
<br>
<sup class="versenum">10</sup> All mine enemies shall be put to shame and sore troubled:
<br>
They shall turn back, they shall be put to shame suddenly.
<br>
<br>
</div>
