<a href = "#chapter/book10--chapter9.txt"><span class="icon icon-forward"></span></a></p>
<div class = "bible">
<sup class="versenum">1</sup> And after this it came to pass, that David (Dawad) smote the Philistines, and subdued them: and David (Dawad) took the bridle of the mother city out of the hand of the Philistines.
<br>
<sup class="versenum">2</sup> And he smote Moab, and measured them with the line, making them to lie down on the ground; and he measured two lines to put to death, and one full line to keep alive.
<br>
 And the Moabites became servants to David (Dawad), and brought tribute.
<br>
<sup class="versenum">3</sup> David (Dawad) smote also Hadadezer the son of Rehob, king of Zobah, as he went to recover his dominion at the River.
<br>
<sup class="versenum">4</sup> And David (Dawad) took from him a thousand and seven hundred horsemen, and twenty thousand footmen: and David (Dawad) hocked all the chariot horses, but reserved of them for a hundred chariots.
<br>
<sup class="versenum">5</sup> And when the Syrians of Damascus came to succor Hadadezer king of Zobah, David (Dawad) smote of the Syrians two and twenty thousand men.
<br>
<sup class="versenum">6</sup> Then David (Dawad) put garrisons in Syria of Damascus; and the Syrians became servants to David (Dawad), and brought tribute.
<br>
 And تتراجراماتون gave victory to David (Dawad) whithersoever he went.
<br>
<sup class="versenum">7</sup> And David (Dawad) took the shields of gold that were on the servants of Hadadezer, and brought them to Jerusalem.
<br>
<sup class="versenum">8</sup> And from Betah and from Berothai, cities of Hadadezer, king David (Dawad) took exceeding much brass.
<br>
<sup class="versenum">9</sup> And when Toi king of Hamath heard that David (Dawad) had smitten all the host of Hadadezer, 
<br>
<sup class="versenum">10</sup> then Toi sent Joram his son unto king David (Dawad), to salute him, and to bless him, because he had fought against Hadadezer and smitten him: for Hadadezer had wars with Toi.
<br>
 And Joram brought with him vessels of silver, and vessels of gold, and vessels of brass: 
<br>
<sup class="versenum">11</sup> These also did king David (Dawad) dedicate unto تتراجراماتون, with the silver and gold that he dedicated of all the nations which he subdued; 
<br>
<sup class="versenum">12</sup> of Syria, and of Moab, and of the children of Ammon, and of the Philistines, and of Amalek, and of the spoil of Hadadezer, son of Rehob, king of Zobah.
<br>
<sup class="versenum">13</sup> And David (Dawad) gat him a name when he returned from smiting the Syrians in the Valley of Salt, even eighteen thousand men.
<br>
<sup class="versenum">14</sup> And he put garrisons in Edom; throughout all Edom put he garrisons, and all the Edomites became servants to David (Dawad).
<br>
 And تتراجراماتون gave victory to David (Dawad) whithersoever he went.
<br>
<sup class="versenum">15</sup> And David (Dawad) reigned over all Israel; and David (Dawad) executed justice and righteousness unto all his people.
<br>
<sup class="versenum">16</sup> And Joab the son of Zeruiah was over the host; and Jehoshaphat the son of Ahilud was recorder; 
<br>
<sup class="versenum">17</sup> and Zadok the son of Ahitub, and Ahimelech the son of Abiathar, were priests; and Seraiah was scribe; 
<br>
<sup class="versenum">18</sup> and Benaiah the son of Jehoiada was over the Cherethites and the Pelethites; and David’s sons were chief ministers.
<br>
<br>
</div>
